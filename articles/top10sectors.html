An Unstoppable Trend

Five particular sectors (Renewable Energies, AI & ML, Biotech & Pharma, Transportation, IT & Cybersecurity) appear to be almost certainly promising for the future. The reasons are so evident that they don't require a surprising demonstration. However, a thorough analysis of each of these sectors is highly relevant. Three other sectors can potentially yield exponentially high returns; Space Exploration, Sustainable Agriculture and Ed Tech & Remote Learning.

Other factors come into play

  • › The same phenomenon as the dot-com bubble can happen, which was characterized by a first stage of general euphoria and optimism leading to a bubble, followed by the bubble burst, before finally having the actual revolution that we know very well.
  • › We didn't mention the military industry, although it will likely continue to grow, albeit slowly and over the long term, in light of the diminishing influence of the American superpower, escalating global tensions, and the increasing scarcity of resources.
  • › Stock picking will be crucial, as many companies in these sectors are overpriced or underpriced.
  • › Finally, some sectors will remain essential and are not ready to disappear. If they are currently flying under the radar, it is not unlikely that they will develop in parallel with technological advancements and still emerge as dominant sectors.

Timing is Key

In conclusion, knowing that a sector will grow or decline in the future is helpful, but understanding when and to what extent is also important if you want to maximize your returns. Regarding the declining sectors (fossil energies, physical businesses...), remember that companies in those segments can definitely change their trajectory and adapt their business models. Don't bury them too quickly and easily.
